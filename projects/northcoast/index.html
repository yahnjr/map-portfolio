    • Wildlife Injuries in the North Coast

    Skills used: Raster and vector analysis, crime hotspot analysis, project design and leading, policy recommendations

    In this project, which I led for the PCC GIS Club, we contacted the Wildlife Center of the North Coast in Astoria, OR. They shared their patient intake data. The series of maps looks for areas where a disproportionate amount of animals are being injured through the use of clustering, crime analysis, and creating road segments. The areas surrounding these hot spots were analyzed to find sections of road with surrounding forest or wetland habitat, and several sites for possible wildlife tunnels or bridges were contained. This map was shared with the wildlife center and the local government in the region, and hopefully policies can be implemented to reduce the instances of injured wildlife and keep our natural resources safe.     About Me

I believe all aspects of bringing spatial data to people, including cartography, application development, and communication, are all important aspects of what makes GIS special.
