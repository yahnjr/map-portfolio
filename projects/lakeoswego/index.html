    • Oregon White Oaks in Lake Oswego

    Skills used:

    A mapping project undertaken in collaboration with the Oswego Lake Watershed Council, examining the Oregon white oaks in the area. A focus was placed on known oak trees that exist in neighborhoods and other green spaces that are not considered parts of continuous oak habitat, and the value they can provide local wildlife and ecology. As part of this project, several different analyses were carried out including establishing connective habitat zones, joining to tax lots to establish address locations for oak trees, habitat suitability surveys for potential unmapped oaks, and oak tree size classification. A web app and story map were created to allow the final products to be used by both members of the council and the public, along with a Survey123 form so more oak tree points could be added into the map in real time.
